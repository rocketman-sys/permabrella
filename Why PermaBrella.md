# Why PermaBrella Exists

PermaBrella exists to help strengthen local food security, practical connection, and community resilience in the Northern Rivers.

Across the region, there is already a huge amount of knowledge, goodwill, skill, land, experience, and willingness to help. But much of it is scattered. People often do not know where to look, who is doing what, what is available, or how to get involved.

PermaBrella is here to help reduce that fragmentation.

It brings together people, events, offerings, land opportunities, practical knowledge, and community initiatives so it becomes easier to participate, share resources, and build useful local relationships.

The aim is not just to hold information.

The real aim is to help create more real-world outcomes:
- more food being grown locally
- more people involved in regenerative action
- more sharing of skills, seeds, tools, and opportunities
- stronger local connection and reciprocity
- greater resilience in the face of disruption

PermaBrella is grounded in a simple idea:

A healthier region is one that can better connect its own people, capacities, and resources.

This platform is one small attempt to support that.

## What We Care About

- Practical local action
- Food-growing capacity
- Sharing of skills and resources
- Clear and trustworthy information
- Community participation
- Mutual support and resilience

---

## What We Are Building Toward

We want PermaBrella to help the Northern Rivers become:
- more connected
- more capable
- more food-secure
- more participatory
- more resilient

Not through abstraction, but through practical, local, grounded action.

---

## A Note on Human and AI Content

PermaBrella may include both human and AI-assisted content.

Where AI is used, we aim to make that visible. Human observation, lived experience, and local knowledge remain central.

The role of digital tools should be to support connection, clarity, and action - not to replace grounded human judgment.
